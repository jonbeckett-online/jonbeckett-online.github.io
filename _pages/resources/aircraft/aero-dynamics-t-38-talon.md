# Northrop T-38 Talon

## The Real Aircraft

The Northrop T-38 Talon is a twin-engine supersonic jet trainer that has served as the USAF's primary advanced and supersonic pilot training platform since 1961. It remains in service over 60 years later, having trained thousands of pilots including NASA astronauts.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Manufacturer:** Northrop Corporation
- **First Flight:** April 10, 1959
- **Introduction:** March 1961
- **Type:** Two-seat supersonic trainer
- **Engines:** 2Ã— General Electric J85-GE-5 turbojets
- **Max Speed:** 1,381 km/h (Mach 1.3)
- **Range:** 1,835 km (1,140 miles)
- **Ceiling:** 16,800 m (55,000 ft)

With over 1,100 built, the T-38 is the world's first supersonic trainer and has accumulated more than 13 million flight hours. Its clean design was derived from the F-5 Freedom Fighter program and offers exceptional performance, responsiveness, and reliability.

## The Simulation

The **Aero Dynamics T-38 Talon** brings this iconic supersonic trainer to Microsoft Flight Simulator with attention to authentic flight characteristics.

**Features:**
- Detailed exterior and interior modeling
- Supersonic flight capability
- Authentic USAF and NASA liveries
- Responsive handling true to the real aircraft
- Compatible with MSFS 2020/2024
