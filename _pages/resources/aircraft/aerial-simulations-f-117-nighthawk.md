# Lockheed F-117 Nighthawk

## The Real Aircraft

The Lockheed F-117 Nighthawk was the world's first operational stealth aircraft, developed by Lockheed's legendary Skunk Works division. Though designated a "fighter," it was primarily a precision ground-attack aircraft that revolutionized modern warfare.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Manufacturer:** Lockheed Aeronautics
- **First Flight:** June 18, 1981
- **Introduction:** October 1983
- **Retired:** April 22, 2008
- **Length:** 20.09 m (65 ft 11 in)
- **Wingspan:** 13.20 m (43 ft 4 in)
- **Engines:** 2Ã— General Electric F404-F1D2 turbofans
- **Max Speed:** 1,130 km/h (617 knots)
- **Range:** 1,720 km (930 nmi)

The F-117's distinctive angular stealth design was the result of early computer-aided design technology and gave it an unprecedented ability to avoid radar detection. It saw combat in Panama, the Gulf War, Kosovo, and Iraq, achieving remarkable success in striking high-value targets.

## The Simulation

The **Aerial Simulations F-117 Nighthawk** brings this iconic stealth aircraft to Microsoft Flight Simulator, allowing pilots to experience one of aviation's most distinctive designs.

**Features:**
- Authentic angular stealth geometry
- Detailed cockpit with period-accurate instrumentation
- Realistic fly-by-wire flight characteristics
- Multiple squadron liveries
- Compatible with MSFS 2020/2024

**Developer:** Aerial Simulations
