# North American P-51D Mustang

## The Real Aircraft

The North American P-51 Mustang is widely regarded as the best all-round single-seat piston fighter of World War II. Its ability to escort heavy bombers deep into Germany changed the course of the air war in Europe.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Manufacturer:** North American Aviation
- **First Flight:** October 26, 1940 (NA-73X)
- **Introduction:** 1942
- **Type:** Single-seat fighter/escort
- **Engine:** Packard V-1650-7 Merlin (1,490 hp)
- **Max Speed:** 703 km/h (437 mph)
- **Range:** 2,755 km (1,650 miles) with drop tanks
- **Armament:** 6Ã— .50 cal machine guns

The combination of the Mustang airframe with the Rolls-Royce Merlin engine created an unbeatable formula. P-51s destroyed nearly 5,000 enemy aircraft in aerial combat and another 4,100 on the ground. It remains one of the most iconic and recognizable warbirds today.
