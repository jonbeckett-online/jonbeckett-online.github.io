# Globe Swift

## The Real Aircraft

The Globe GC-1 Swift is an American two-seat, single-engine, all-metal low-wing monoplane that was produced by Globe Aircraft and later Temco after World War II. Known for its streamlined design and sporty performance, the Swift became a favorite among pilots who valued style and speed.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Manufacturer:** Globe Aircraft / Temco
- **First Flight:** 1942
- **Production:** 1946-1951
- **Type:** Two-seat sport aircraft
- **Engine:** Continental C-145-2 (145 hp)
- **Cruise Speed:** 225 km/h (140 mph)
- **Range:** 885 km (550 miles)
- **Ceiling:** 4,877 m (16,000 ft)

With retractable landing gear and clean lines, the Swift offered performance that rivaled aircraft costing much more. About 1,520 were built, and the type remains popular with vintage aircraft enthusiasts today. The Swift's distinctive appearance—bubble canopy, retractable gear, and swept tail—make it instantly recognizable.
