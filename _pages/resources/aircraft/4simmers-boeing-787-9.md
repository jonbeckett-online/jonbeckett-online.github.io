# Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner

## The Real Aircraft

The Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner is a long-range, mid-size widebody twin-engine jet airliner developed by Boeing Commercial Airplanes. First flying in 2013, the -9 variant is a stretched version of the original 787-8, offering increased passenger capacity and range.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Manufacturer:** Boeing
- **First Flight:** September 17, 2013
- **Introduction:** August 2014 (Air New Zealand)
- **Length:** 62.8 m (206 ft)
- **Wingspan:** 60.1 m (197 ft)
- **Range:** 14,140 km (7,635 nmi)
- **Cruise Speed:** Mach 0.85 (903 km/h)
- **Passengers:** 296 (typical two-class)
- **Engines:** 2Ã— GEnx-1B or Rolls-Royce Trent 1000

The 787 family revolutionized commercial aviation with its extensive use of composite materials (approximately 50% of the airframe), resulting in improved fuel efficiency, lower operating costs, and enhanced passenger comfort features including larger windows, higher cabin humidity, and lower cabin altitude.

## The Simulation

The **4Simmers Boeing 787-9** brings the Dreamliner to Microsoft Flight Simulator with a focus on visual fidelity and accessibility. This add-on provides pilots with the opportunity to experience one of aviation's most modern widebody aircraft.

**Features:**
- Detailed exterior and interior modeling
- Custom cockpit with functional displays
- Multiple livery options
- Compatible with MSFS 2020/2024

**Developer:** 4Simmers
